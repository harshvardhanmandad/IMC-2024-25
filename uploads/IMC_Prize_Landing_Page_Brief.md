# IMC 2024-25 — Complete Context for Landing Page Design

This document provides everything needed to design the IMC prize announcement landing page. Assume zero prior knowledge. Read top to bottom.

---

## Part 1: What is the International Math Challenge?

The International Math Challenge (IMC) is an online math competition for kids aged 9–13, organized by MathAI. It tests mathematical thinking across multiple formats — not just calculations, but logic, estimation, pattern recognition, and creative problem-solving.

**Edition:** 2024-25 (this is the results & prizes announcement)
**Organizer:** MathAI
**Mode:** Online
**Target Age:** 9–13 years

---

## Part 2: Who Participated?

Students participated in **3 grade categories**:

| Grade | Who |
|-------|-----|
| Grade 3 | 3rd graders (age ~8-9) |
| Grade 4 | 4th graders (age ~9-10) |
| Grade 5 | 5th graders (age ~10-11) |

Each grade is treated as a completely independent competition. A Grade 3 student only competes against other Grade 3 students. Rankings, prizes, and everything else is applied separately per grade.

---

## Part 3: What Did They Compete In?

Every student attempted **5 different challenges**. Each challenge tests a different mathematical skill:

| # | Challenge | What It Tests |
|---|-----------|---------------|
| 1 | Mental Maths | Speed and accuracy in mental arithmetic — no pen, no paper, just brain |
| 2 | Patterns & Logic | Spotting mathematical patterns, sequences, logical reasoning |
| 3 | Estimations | Number sense — how close can you guess without calculating exactly? |
| 4 | Riddles | Mathematical riddles that require lateral thinking |
| 5 | Puzzles & Games | Strategic thinking, game theory, spatial puzzles |

These 5 challenges are the same across all 3 grades (difficulty is adjusted per grade).

## Part 4: How Rankings Work

There are **two types of rankings**, and this is important to understand because prizes are different for each:

### Type A: Overall Ranking

- Takes a student's combined score across ALL 5 challenges
- Ranks them against everyone else in their grade
- **Top 100 students per grade** are ranked (Rank 1 through Rank 100)
- This is the "main" ranking — the one that determines the biggest prizes
- Total across all grades: 300 students ranked (100 × 3 grades)

### Type B: Challenge-Wise Ranking

- Takes a student's score in ONE specific challenge
- Ranks them against everyone else in their grade for THAT challenge
- **Top 20 students per challenge per grade** are ranked
- There are 5 challenges × 3 grades = 15 separate challenge leaderboards
- Total slots: 300 (20 × 5 × 3)

### Can a student appear in both?

**Yes.** A student who is strong overall will likely also be a topper in at least one individual challenge. For example:
- Aarav (Grade 4) could be Rank 3 Overall AND Rank 1 in Mental Maths
- He receives prizes from BOTH rankings (stacking rules apply — see Part 7)

## Part 6: Prize Structure

Prizes are split into two types:
1. **Physical Prizes** — tangible products shipped to the student's home
2. **Program Credits** — free months on MathAI's "10x Speed & Logic Program" (an online math program that costs ₹799/month)

### Who Gets Physical Prizes?

Only the TOP tiers get physical items. Specifically:
- Overall Rank 1–10 (30 students total)
- Challenge Rank 1 (15 students total)
- **Maximum 45 physical prize shipments**

Everyone else (Rank 11–100 overall, Challenge Rank 2–20) gets program credits only — no physical item.

### Who Gets Program Credits?

**Every single ranked student** gets some amount of free program access. The higher your rank, the more months free.

## Part 9: Program Credit Details

**What is the program?**
- Name: 10x Speed & Logic Program
- Provider: MathAI (same company running the competition)
- Monthly price: ₹799/month
- It's an ongoing math enrichment program focused on speed and logical thinking

**How credits work:**
- Winners get X months of free access
- Credit granularity: 1 month, 45 days, 2 months, 2.5 months, 3 months
- Maximum credit: 3 months per student (hard cap)
- Credits activate on the ceremony date
- After free months expire, student can continue as a paying subscriber

**Stacking cap:**
- If a student qualifies for credits from multiple rankings, they DON'T get unlimited months
- Cap is 3 months maximum regardless of how many prizes they win
- Example: Overall Rank 2 (3 months) + Challenge Rank 1 (2 months) = 3 months total, NOT 5 months

## Part 10: Video Bonus & Engagement

Students are asked to submit a short video (30-60 seconds) as part of claiming their prize.

| Tier | Video Requirement | What Happens If They Don't Submit |
|------|------|------|
| Overall Rank 1–10 | **Mandatory** | Cannot claim physical prize without video |
| Challenge Rank 1 | **Mandatory** | Cannot claim physical prize without video |
| Overall Rank 11–50 | **Incentivized** | They still get their credits, but miss out on +15 days bonus |
| Everyone else | **Optional** | No penalty, just encouraged |

**Video bonus:** Students in Rank 11–50 who submit a video get 15 extra days of free program credit.

## Part 11: Stacking Rules (When a Student Wins Multiple Times)

Since students can rank in BOTH overall and challenge-wise:

**Physical prizes:** STACK. Student gets both items.
- Example: Rank 1 Overall + Challenge Rank 1 in Riddles = iPad + Mini Printer (both shipped)

**Program credits:** CAPPED at 3 months.
- Example: Rank 1 Overall gives 3 months + Challenge Rank 1 gives 2 months = 3 months total (not 5)

**Certificates:** Get separate certificates for each ranking achieved.

## Part 14: Prize Claim & Delivery Process

1. **Results announced** — Already done. Students know their ranks.
2. **Prize claim form sent** — To families of physical prize winners. Collects: delivery address, consent for video usage.
3. **Video submission** — Students record 30-60 sec video. Deadline: 5 days before ceremony.
4. **Virtual ceremony** — Live event (~65 min). Countdown reveals, winner videos played, live interaction with Top 3.
5. **Program credits activated** — On ceremony day, all eligible students get access.
6. **Physical prizes shipped** — Within 7 days after ceremony.

## Part 15: Landing Page Design Notes

### What to emphasize:
- The aspirational prizes up top (iPad, Kindle) — this is what draws attention
- That EVERY ranked student wins something (even Rank 100 gets program credits)
- The 5 unique challenge categories (makes the competition feel comprehensive, not just "another math test")
- Total value ₹7,71,000+ (use this on the landing page — it's a marketing context)

### What NOT to show:
- Per-unit prices of prizes (don't show ₹2,068 on the public page — it cheapens the prize)
- The ₹2,50,000 budget (internal number — use ₹7,71,000+ total value instead)
- Stacking cap math (put in FAQ section, not main page)
- "Mandatory video" language (frame it as "share your winning moment" not as a requirement)

### Suggested page structure:
1. **Hero** — "International Math Challenge 2024-25 — Winners & Prizes"
2. **The 5 Challenges** — Visual cards with icons for each challenge type
3. **Prize Showcase** — Big visual cards for physical prize tiers (Rank 1, 2, 3, 4-10, Challenge Rank 1)
4. **Every Winner Gets Rewarded** — Program credit tiers (show that Rank 11-100 and Challenge Rank 2-20 all get free program access)
5. **How to Claim** — Step-by-step: check rank → submit video → fill form → attend ceremony → receive prize
6. **FAQ** — Stacking rules, credit cap, shipping timeline, video specs

### Tone:
- Celebratory, exciting, achievement-focused
- Kid-friendly but parent-approved
- "You earned this" energy
- Not corporate or overly formal
